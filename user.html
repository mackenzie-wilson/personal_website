Constructing User Personas

April 20th, 2023

Product Management | Design | Development

Resources Used
The Product Book: How to Become a Great Product Manager (2nd Edition) - Product School
Product Management in Practice: A Real-World Guide to the Key Connective Role of the 21st Century - Matt Lemay

"User personas are composite profiles of user needs and behaviours designed to get us out of our own heads and focus on the people for whom we are truly building."

What is a User Persona?

"We can take the various common traits we care about in our potential customers and abstract them out into a persona. A persona is a fictional, typical customer, and defining key personas lets you segment your customers by highlighting the things your customers care about that are relevant to your product. Personas are tools to help you understand your customers, they are not actual end customers" - taken from The Product Book.

Since we are in the early stages of development, we don't know much about our typical customer and therefore must rely on assumptions. These assumptions are essentially hypotheses about our users' needs and behaviours that will be tested and altered to better fit reality as we continuously talk to them. The user personas will then be used to differentiate who is receiving value from our product requirements through the use of user stories, a practice that will be covered in the next blog post.

Our User Segments

We have identified 2 user segments: Restaurant Owners & Restaurant Goers

Segment #1: Restaurant Owners - Khalid Hoover

Khalid Hoover is a middle-aged restaurant owner that lives and operates his pub in downtown Ottawa with the help of his family.

Actions, Motivations, & Pains
What do I do?
  • Operate and own a restaurant
  • Develop the restaurant deals, promotions, offers or specials
  • Overall pricing strategy
Why do I do it?
  • Freedom of being my own boss
  • Put food on the table for my family
  • Get customers in our seats
What do I want?
  • To advertise my restaurant and its deals
  • To turn a profit
  • Bring in new customers
  • Increase customer loyalty
What's stopping me?
  • High advertising costs
  • High operating costs
  • Inconsistent customer turnout
Values
What convinces me?
  • Advertising platform is cheap enough to use
  • I can see analytics and a return on investment
  • The service is easy to use
What or who informs me?
  • Customers
  • Competitors
  • Food and beverage suppliers
  • Emails
  • Cold calls
  • Word of mouth
Context
Where am I?
  • Ottawa
  • Downtown
What's my day-to-day?
  • Restaurant operations
  • Managing employees
  • Earning money to feed my family
  • Coordinating with suppliers

Segment #2: Restaurant Goer - Jaydon Burgess

Jaydon Burgess is a 3rd year Carleton student in the Architecture program. Between a demanding workload and a part time job, Jaydon does not have much time or money to make food at home.

Actions, Motivations, & Pains
What do I do?
  • Work part time as I am a student - limited income
  • Busy with my school schedule - no time to cook
  • Eat while I'm out and about more than cook at home
  • Seek deals before I grocery shop or eat out
  • Look for cheap activities to do with friends
Why do I do it?
  • Want to spend time with friends and have a good time on a limited budget
  • Maintain some semblance of a social life
What do I want?
  • Find and compare deals nearby
  • Convince friends go out with me
  • Not break the bank whenever I go out to eat
What's stopping me?
  • Restaurants are increasing prices as the cost of food goes up
  • Groceries are going up in price and costs valuable time to prepare and cook
  • I don't know what restaurant deals are near me
Values
What convinces me?
  • Good deals
  • Guarenteed savings
  • Opportunity to spend time with friends
What or who informs me?
  • Restaurant social media posts
Context
Where am I?
  • Trapped at home
  • Tight living conditions with other students/roomates
  • Unable to cook in crowded kitchen
What's my day-to-day?
  • Studying for school
  • Part time job
