Business Model Design: Applying the Business Model Canvas

March 26th, 2023

Product Management | Design | Development

Resources Used
The Product Book: How to Become a Great Product Manager (2nd Edition) - Product School

"The Business Model Canvas provides a way to look at all aspects of your company with a focus on people, values and revenue, and filling it out requires you to list facts and hypotheses."

Contents of the Business Model Canvas

Key partners: Who externally makes the business model work?
Investors
Carleton University
Hatch program
Crowdfunding and other startup raising methods
Resturant and bar owners/managers
Franchise owners
Restaurant corporations

Key Activities: What are the tasks must be perfomred to deliver value?
Onboarding restaurants
Continuous development of platform/application and services
Advertising to userbase through social media and other opportunities

Key Resources: What are the physical, financial, and intellectual resources the company requires?
Hybrid mobile application (Android + iOs)
Web application
Social media (advertising)
Servers for hosting tech
Carleton University/Hatch program
Mentors
Founders' personal networks

Value Propositions: For a given user persona, what value does using the product bring?
Free app for users to find deals and save money on nights out
Restaurants have an affordable and efficient platform to market their deals
People need affordable food options as prices have been rising

Customer Relationships: What relationships need to be established?
Resturants and their managers/owners looking for affordable local marketing platform
Matching users to nerby restaurants with deals

Channels: How does the company reach personas to deliver value?
Directly approaching restaurants to validate the idea that we can provide value by way of marketing services
Advertising across social media network to build user base
Onboarding restaurants through B2B marketing

Customer Segments: Who are the key personas being served?
Non-chain restaurants that want more people in their seats
Families and students looking to sae money on nights out/dining in
New restaurants that need to establish a loyal customer base

Cost Structure: What are the business costs?
Server costs
Paying founders and early-stage employees in equity
Advertising costs
Administrative costs (i.e. accounting)
Wages
Revenue Streams: Money in minus money out
Forego monetization in early stages for increasing users/restaurants
Monthly subscription for restaurants with a tiered pay structure
