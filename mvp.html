Building CheapNite's MVP: A Journey to Delivering Value Quickly

October 7th, 2023

Product Management | Design | Development


Introduction

In the world of app development, one term frequently thrown around is "MVP." But what exactly is an MVP? MVP stands for Minimum Viable Product, and it's a crucial stage in the development of any new software or app. Essentially, an MVP is a stripped-down version of your product that includes only the most essential features to solve a specific problem or meet a particular need. It's the first step in bringing your vision to life while minimizing risks and maximizing efficiency.

CheapNite - A Concept and Vision

Our journey begins with CheapNite, an app designed to help users discover nearby deals offered by restaurants. These deals could include discounts on food, drinks, and even information about exciting events like karaoke nights. Restaurants pay a fee to promote these deals, making it a win-win situation for both businesses and app users. The concept behind CheapNite was enticing, but realizing it required careful planning, prioritization, and a dash of creativity.

Prioritizing Features

Building the MVP for CheapNite was a thrilling adventure filled with decisions, trade-offs, and hard work. The first challenge was to decide which features to include in the initial release. Our wish list was long and promising, with ideas like geolocation, in-app directions to reach the establishments, and even a paid subscription model for restaurants.

However, due to time constraints, we had to make some tough decisions. We chose to prioritize the most critical features that aligned with our core value proposition – helping users find the best nearby deals. This meant that geolocation and in-app directions, while valuable, had to be deferred to future updates. The paid subscription model for restaurants also took a back seat in the MVP, as it required additional development time.

Design Considerations

Design is an integral part of any app's user experience. While we had a vision for how our app should look and feel, we also had to balance this with the need to launch quickly. As a result, certain design aspects, such as the detailed view of a deal when clicked and the overall appearance of the home page, had to be simplified or postponed.

The Race Against Time

UThe primary reason behind our time constraints was a strategic marketing initiative. We decided to target university students, a key demographic for CheapNite, during their first week of university, also known as Frosh Week. This presented a golden opportunity to introduce our app to a large and enthusiastic audience, but it also meant that we had a strict deadline to meet.

Launching Our MVP

In our next blog post, we will delve into the exciting phase of launching our MVP during Frosh Week. We'll explore the strategies we employed, the challenges we faced, and the valuable lessons we learned. Stay tuned for the follow-up article to see how CheapNite fared in its initial release and how it resonated with our target demographic.

Conclusion

Building an MVP is a crucial step in the development of any app or software. It allows you to test your concept, deliver value quickly, and gather user feedback for future improvements. While time constraints forced us to prioritize certain features and design elements, the journey of creating CheapNite's MVP was a rewarding experience. In the fast-paced world of app development, sometimes it's more important to launch and iterate than to wait for perfection. Keep an eye out for our next blog post, where we share the thrilling tale of launching CheapNite during Frosh Week and the insights gained from our users' experiences.
