Designing the Minimum Viable Product: Mockups

July 11th, 2023

Product Management| Design | Development


Introduction

Previous posts, discussed crucial topics such as Business Model Design, Constructing User Personas, and Writing User Stories using the INVEST and SMART Approaches. In this installment, we'll dive into the importance of designing app mockups from a User Experience perspective.

Breaking Down User Stories

User stories play a vital role in understanding the needs and expectations of your target audience. By empathizing with your users, you can create mockups that fulfill their desires and goals. Through user-centric design, you can deliver a seamless and intuitive experience that aligns with their expectations.

Designing with Whiteboarding Software: Miro

To efficiently design the mockup screens and visualize how the app will look on a phone, consider using whiteboarding software like Miro. Miro allows for collaborative and interactive brainstorming sessions, making it an ideal tool for designing app mockups. Create a digital canvas and invite your team members to participate in real-time. With Miro, you can easily add pre-designed UI elements, draw wireframes, and arrange the screens to visualize the app's flow.

Designing the Loading Page

The loading page sets the tone for the user's experience. Ensure it is visually appealing and incorporates visual elements that align with your app's branding. For example, you could enhance the loading page with cute food-based animations, such as animated fruits or playful cooking utensils. These animations can bring a touch of delight and anticipation to the loading process, making it more engaging for users.

Sign Up Pages, Main Page for Users, Restaurant Main Page, Profile Page, and Support Page

Utilize Miro to design the various screens of your app, including the Sign Up Pages, Main Page for Users, Restaurant Main Page, Profile Page, and Support Page. Experiment with different layouts, elements, and interactions to create an intuitive and visually appealing user interface. Collaborate with your team to gather feedback and make iterative improvements, ensuring that the mockups align with the user stories and deliver a delightful user experience.

Conclusion

Designing app mockups with a focus on User Experience is integral to the success of your restaurant marketing app. By understanding the app's purpose, breaking down user stories, and utilizing tools like Miro to design mockups, you can create an app that prioritizes user satisfaction and engagement. Stay tuned for our next blog post in the series, where we'll explore the importance of app usability testing to further optimize the user experience of your restaurant marketing app.
